Welcome to the latest edition of ACM Books Alert. As part of this edition, we are pleased to announce new titles and a new ACM Books Collection!

First, ACM Books Collection I welcomes a few new titles, including:

Hardness of Approximation Between P and NP,
by Aviad Rubinstein

The author presents several scenarios to test whether an efficient algorithm exists for finding approximate Nash equilibrium (the central solution concept in Game Theory), and provides a compelling argument that finding an approximate Nash equilibrium via computing is an intractable problem.

ACM is also excited to announce the availability of ACM Books Collection II! Similar to Collection I, Collection II will also have 25 titles upon completion and will be available via a one-time purchase model with perpetual access rights. The first two titles are now available, including:

Heterogeneous Computing,
by Mohamed Zahran

This work discusses the various forms of heterogeneity in computing (i.e., the fundamentally different systems that co-exist), and the challenges and opportunities that exist because of them.

Conversational UX Design,
by Robert J. Moore and Raphael Arar

This new title adapts formal knowledge from the field of Conversation Analysis to aid in the design of natural language interfaces, allowing human-computer interaction via conversation to be more effective.
